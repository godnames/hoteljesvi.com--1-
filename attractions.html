Mount Mary Church

Mount Mary Church named Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount is one of the most famous Roman Catholic churches in all of Mumbai and visiting it is a must. It was built in the 16th century and has stood erect for over 100 years. During the Bandra Fair, the entire area is decorated with festoons and buntings. Many pitch up stalls to sell religious articles, roasted grams, snacks, and sweets.

Sanjay Gandhi National Park

Spreading over an area of 87sq.km of land, it is the most visited national park in Asia and the best spot for picnics & tours with families and loved ones. Romancing local Mumbai couples also spend their time together with the National Park. This park can surely be a family entertainer with all sorts of fun activities; also you can watch the big cats (Lion, Tiger, and Leopard) of the park at the safari cage. Bating also makes a lot of fun in the park with its artificial lake.

Powai Lake

Powai Lake is an artificial lake, situated in Mumbai, in the Powai valley, where a Powai village with a cluster of huts existed. The city suburb called Powai shares its name with the lake. Powai Lake is located downstream of the Vihar lake on the Mithi river. Powai Lake has gone through many stages of water quality degradation. The lake water which used to supply drinking water for Mumbai has been declared unfit to drink. The lake still remains a tourist attraction. The MSAA has revised its constitution "to actively care for, clean, develop, maintain, and beautify the Environment at Powai Lake."

Jogeshwari Caves

The Jogeshwari Caves are some of the earliest Buddhist cave temples sculptures located in the Mumbai suburb of Jogeshwari, India. The caves date back to 520 to 550 CE. These caves belong to the Hindus daily Jogeshwari. According to historian and scholar Walter Spink. Jogeshwari is the earliest major cave temple in India. The caves are located off the Western Express Highway and are surrounded by encroachments. The caves are accessed through a long flight of stairs into the main hall of this cavernous space. It has many pillars and a lingam at the end. Idols of Dattatreya, Hanuman, and Ganesh line the walls. There are also relics of two doormen. The cave also has a murti and footprints of goddess Jogeshwari, whom the area is named after. The goddess is considered a Kuladevi by some Marathi people and also worshipped by some migrant groups from Gujarat.

Film City

Your trip to Mumbai will not be complete unless you visit the Film City. Here you can get a glimpse into the day-to-day life of the Hindi film industry – Bollywood. The Film City complex contains over ten studios as well as a variety of ready-to-shoot outdoor locations. Temples, playgrounds, and train sequence arrangements are all available here. Mumbai Film City is approximately 9 km away from Hotel J esvi. During your visit, you may also be able to witness a live Hindi movie shoot. Regional language films are also shot there.

Versova Beach

Versova Beach, located in Andheri West, has a rocky boundary, high tides, and is one of Mumbai's cleanest and least visited beaches. Most likely, families and couples spend their evenings at Versova beach watching the sunset, riding a horse, and taking a ferry to Madh Island from Versova Jetty. The last end of the beach to the jetty is home to the well-known fishing community of Koli, dig some tasty seafood at one of the nearby cafes. True photographers will be smitten by the beach at dawn and dusk.

Haji Ali Dargah

Haji Ali Dargah is a popular religious site in Mumbai, with millions of visitors each year and thousands of pilgrims each week. It is the most well-known Holy Sufi Saint Landmark in Mumbai, located near the Mumbai seashore and the Arabian Sea. It is a complex that includes the tomb of Muslim Sufi Saint Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari (R.A.) as well as a mosque next to the dargah. The white marble structure has white domes and minarets, similar to Indo-Islamic and Mughal architecture of the time. Millions of devotees visit the dargah from all over the world, without regard for caste, creed, or religion, and offer prayers for the fulfillment of their wishes through the mercy and favor of the Sufi Saint Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari (R.A.).
